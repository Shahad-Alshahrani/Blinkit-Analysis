## 📌 Project Overview

This project features a comprehensive Sales and Inventory Analysis Dashboard for Blinkit, a leading quick-commerce platform in India. The goal is to evaluate sales performance, customer satisfaction, and inventory throughput to uncover actionable insights and identify strategic opportunities for operational optimization.

## 📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

* **Total Sales:** $1.20M in revenue, demonstrating a strong and established market presence.
* **Average Sales:** $141 per order, showcasing a highly competitive order value for a quick-commerce platform.
* **Order Volume:** 8,523 total items sold, reflecting robust supply chain operations and high velocity.
* **Customer Satisfaction:** An average rating of 3.9 out of 5.0, indicating a healthy baseline of customer trust with room for competitive growth.

## 📈 Product & Demand Analysis

* **Fat Content Preferences:** Low Fat items are the dominant revenue driver, generating $776.32K (roughly 64% of sales) compared to $425.36K for Regular products. This signifies a highly health-conscious customer base.
* **Top-Performing Categories:** Fruits & Vegetables and Snack Foods lead Blinkit's revenue stream, tying for the top spot at $0.18M each, followed closely by Household items ($0.14M).
* **Lower-Performing Segments:** Specialized or niche categories generate the lowest revenue, with Breakfast items and Starchy Foods at $0.02M each, and Seafood at the bottom with $0.01M.

## 🌍 Regional & Outlet Trends

* **Geographic Funnel:** Tier 3 locations represent the absolute peak of volume, bringing in $472.13K. Tier 2 follows at $393.15K, while premium Tier 1 metro markets capture the lowest revenue share at $336.40K.
* **Widespread Wellness:** The 64% to 36% ratio favoring Low Fat products remains almost identical across all three Tiers, showing that health consciousness is a widespread trend shifting into semi-urban regions.
* **Outlet Scale Efficiency:** Medium ($507.90K) and Small ($444.79K) outlets combined capture over 79% of total sales, proving that hyper-local efficiency and fast dispatch times matter more than massive physical footprint sizes ($248.99K).
* **Establishment Growth:** Outlets founded in 2018 saw a historical peak surge reaching $205K. Older and newer cohorts maintain a tight stability baseline around $130K, showing excellent long-term customer retention.

## 🏪 Outlet Type Performance

* **Supermarket Type 1:** The primary revenue engine for mass throughput, generating $787.55K and moving 5,577 items.
* **Grocery Stores:** A smaller network capturing $151.94K, but achieving the highest Item Visibility at 0.10 (compared to 0.06 for supermarkets), making them ideal for product discovery.
* **Supermarket Type 2 & Type 3:** Perform almost identically, pulling in roughly $131K each with steady ratings of 4.0.

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations

* **Catalog Expansion:** Capitalize on the 64% Low Fat demand by expanding the health and wellness catalog with organic, low-calorie, and functional foods across all tiers, especially in the dominant Tier 3 markets.
* **Optimize Perishable Supply Chains:** Implement dynamic inventory forecasting for high-turnover, perishable categories like Fruits & Vegetables to reduce waste while ensuring 100% peak-hour availability.
* **Revitalize Low-Performing Categories:** Launch targeted morning campaigns (e.g., discounted breakfast bundles) and introduce high-quality, vacuum-sealed seafood branding to overcome freshness concerns.
* **Replicate the "2018 Formula":** Analyze the exact growth drivers behind the 2018 historical peak (e.g., localized dark store formats or marketing plays) to optimize newer outlets experiencing a relative slowdown.
* **Footprint Strategy:** Prioritize medium and small outlet layouts for future expansions to maximize urban density, while leveraging Grocery Stores' high item visibility (0.10) to run hyper-local product placement experiments.
